## Persona 1: The Intentional Solo Visitor

**Name:** Yuki, 29, UX designer at a Taipei tech company

**Concrete context:** Hybrid schedule — 3 days in office, 2 days remote. On remote days she picks a coffee shop to work from. On weekends she treats a slow coffee afternoon as personal reset time. She opens Google Maps on the MRT, tries to pick somewhere by the time she arrives. Usually ends up at the same 3 familiar places because the search results are overwhelming and untrustworthy.

**Primary JTBD:** When I'm heading somewhere new or want a specific offering, help me search in plain language ("quiet pour-over near Zhongshan" or "where has basque cake?"), so I get one trustworthy answer instead of 30 reviews I have to decode.

**What she does today:** Types "咖啡廳 中山" into Google Maps, scrolls through carousel pins, opens 5 tabs, reads 3-star reviews that are 2 years old, gives up and goes to a place she already knows. For specific food items (basque cake, croffles, egg tart), she has no search option at all — she asks on Threads or just hopes.

**Success:** She types what she wants into CafeRoam, picks from 3 results that actually match, and goes. Repeat every week. After 3 months she has stamps from 12 different shops she wouldn't have discovered otherwise.

## Persona 2: The Social Coordinator

**Name:** Jason, 34, marketing manager, organises team outings and weekend friend meetups

**Concrete context:** Every few weeks someone in his friend group or team asks "where should we go Saturday afternoon?" He becomes the de facto coordinator. He needs somewhere with enough seating, the right vibe for the group (relaxed, not too loud), and ideally good enough coffee that the coffee people won't complain. He wastes 30 minutes in a group chat with everyone sending different IG posts and no decision getting made.

**Primary JTBD:** When I need to find a place for a group, help me find somewhere that fits the vibe and send a single convincing link, so I stop playing 30-minute group chat ping-pong.

**What he does today:** Posts in the group chat asking for suggestions, gets 6 different Instagram posts back, googles them individually, still can't decide, picks something random and hopes it works out.

**Success:** He searches CafeRoam, finds 2 options that fit, shares the link directly in the group chat. Decision done in under 5 minutes. The link itself signals "this is a real recommendation, not just a Google Maps pin."

## Persona 3: The Coffee Enthusiast

**Name:** Mei-Ling, 41, freelance translator, dedicated specialty coffee explorer

**Concrete context:** Visits 3–4 different coffee shops a week, deliberately seeking out new places. Follows 10+ coffee IG accounts, reads coffee blogs, keeps a Notes app list of shops she wants to try. She's frustrated that her personal list is scattered across apps with no structure, and discovery still happens through manual blog-reading with no way to search by what she actually wants ("Yirgacheffe single origin, independent roaster, open on Monday").

**Primary JTBD:** When I want to find a specific type of coffee or a shop I haven't tried, help me discover and track my visits, so I can build my own coffee map of Taipei and never repeat a boring shop.

**What she does today:** Reads coffee blogs, DMs IG accounts to ask for recs, maintains a chaotic Notes app list, has no way to track which shops she's already been to.

**Success:** She has stamps from 30+ shops, a list called "specialty only" she actively curates, and she opens CafeRoam before any blog when she wants to find somewhere new. She's the person who shares CafeRoam links on coffee community threads.
